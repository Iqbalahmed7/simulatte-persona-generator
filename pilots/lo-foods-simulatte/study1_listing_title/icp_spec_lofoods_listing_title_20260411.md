# ICP Spec — Study 1: E-Commerce Listing Title Optimisation
**Client:** Lo! Foods — Protein Bread
**Domain:** Functional FMCG / ecommerce (Amazon India)
**Generated:** 2026-04-11

---

## 1. Business Context

**Brand / Product:** Lo! Foods — Protein Bread (200g, Amazon India)

**Business problem:**
Does the presence of the 'keto' keyword in an Amazon listing title suppress click-through and conversion among mainstream health-conscious buyers who are not committed to a ketogenic diet protocol, relative to title framings that lead with protein content, benefit outcomes, or general health positioning?

**Use:** Feed a simulation — personas evaluate 4 listing title variants and rank by click-through intent. Output feeds a Simulatte client case study.

---

## 2. Target Population

**Cohort A — Metro Health-Conscious (No Protocol)**
Urban professionals, 26–42, Metro India. Health-conscious but NOT on a diet protocol. Regular Amazon shoppers.

**Cohort B — Diet Adherents (Keto/Low-Carb)**
Metro India. Active keto or low-carb followers, protocol adherence 0.70+. Macro-trackers and label readers.

**Cohort C — Mainstream E-Commerce Shoppers**
Metro and Tier-1 India, 28–55. Convenience and price primary drivers. Mild health interest, no diet protocol.

## 4. Stimuli

- Variant A — Keto-led: 'Lo! Keto Bread — Zero Carb, High Protein | Gluten Free | 200g'
- Variant B — Benefit-led: 'Lo! High Protein Bread — Low Carb, Guilt-Free | Gluten Free | 200g'
- Variant C — Protein-led: 'Lo! Protein Bread 12g per Slice — Low Carb | Gluten Free | 200g'
- Variant D — Outcome-led: 'Lo! Everyday Healthy Bread — Low Calorie, High Fibre | Gluten Free | 200g'

**Decision scenario:** "You are browsing the bread/bakery category on Amazon India. Which of these four product titles would make you most likely to click through? Rank 1–4 and explain your reasoning."
